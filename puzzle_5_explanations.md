# Puzzle 5 Explanations

Here are the answers and explanations for the 15 riddles found in `Puzzle 5.docx`:

**1. The more you take from it, the bigger it becomes.**
* **Answer:** A hole.
* **Explanation:** When you dig a hole, you are taking away dirt. The more dirt you take away, the bigger the physical hole gets.

**2. The beginning of the end, the end of space and the start of eternity. What is it?**
* **Answer:** The letter 'e'.
* **Explanation:** The letter 'e' is at the beginning of the word "**e**nd", at the end of the word "spac**e**", and at the start of the word "**e**ternity".

**3. Poor people have it, rich people need it and who does not eat it, dies.**
* **Answer:** Nothing.
* **Explanation:** Poor people have *nothing*. Rich people need *nothing*. If you eat *nothing*, you will eventually starve and die.

**4. Two ducks before a duck, two ducks behind a duck, and a duck in de middle. How many ducks are there?**
* **Answer:** Three ducks.
* **Explanation:** They are walking in a single file line (one behind the other). The first duck has two behind it, the last duck has two before it, and the middle duck is sandwiched between the other two.

**5. A country far away is called the country of no idea. In this country there live people but no children, they have houses but no cities and villages. The people have cars, but there are no roads. They people are very clean but you won't find a single dustbin in the country of no idea. Why is it called the country of no idea?**
* **Answer:** Because the things that exist there don't contain the letters "I" or "D" (No I-D-ea).
* **Explanation:** Look at the spelling of the words. The things that are allowed (people, houses, cars, clean) do not contain the letters 'I' or 'D'. The things that are forbidden (ch**i**l**d**ren, c**i**t**i**es, v**i**llages, roa**d**s, **d**ustb**i**n) all contain either an 'I' or a 'D' (or both). 

**6. You can be the last joining person, but you will never be first. What is it?**
* **Answer:** Second place (or a follower).
* **Explanation:** If you are the second person to join a group or finish a race, you are momentarily the "last" person to arrive, but you can never be the "first" because someone else already beat you to it. 

**7. I cannot be felt, seen or touched; Yet I can be found in everybody; My existence is always in debate; Yet I have my own style of music. What Am I?**
* **Answer:** A soul.
* **Explanation:** You can't physically touch a soul, and philosophers and scientists debate its existence. However, "soul music" is a very popular musical genre.

**8. What has four legs and only one foot?**
* **Answer:** A bed.
* **Explanation:** A traditional bed frame has four legs on the floor, but it only has one "foot" (the footboard or the foot of the bed).

**9. It happens once in a minute, twice in a week, and once in a year? What is it?**
* **Answer:** The letter 'e'.
* **Explanation:** Count the 'e's in the words: minüt**e** (1), w**ee**k (2), y**e**ar (1). 

**10. Who makes it, has no need of it. Who buys it, has no use for it. Who uses it can neither see nor feel it.**
* **Answer:** A coffin.
* **Explanation:** The person who builds the coffin sells it. The person who buys it is buying it for a loved one. The person who actually uses it is deceased, so they can't see or feel it.

**11. My love, when I gaze on thy beautiful face, Careering along, yet always in place. The thought has often come into my mind, If I ever shall see thy glorious behind. What am I talking about?**
* **Answer:** The moon.
* **Explanation:** Due to tidal locking, the Moon always shows the exact same "face" to Earth. We never get to see its "behind" (the dark/far side) from the ground.

**12. What holds water yet is full of holes?**
* **Answer:** A sponge.
* **Explanation:** A sponge is porous and covered in holes, but it is designed to absorb and hold liquid.

**13. What belongs to you but others use it more than you do?**
* **Answer:** Your name.
* **Explanation:** It is your identifying name, but your friends and family say it out loud far more often than you do.

**14. Tim owns a pocket knife with five different tools. It has a corkscrew, a bottle opener, tweezers, a screwdriver and something else. What?**
* **Answer:** A knife (or a blade).
* **Explanation:** The object is specifically called a pocket *knife*, so the fifth undocumented tool is the actual knife blade itself.

**15. You've got two jars, one of them fits exactly 5 litres, the other one fits exactly 3 litres. How could you get exactly 4 litres with these two jars? It is allowed to spoil some water.**
* **Answer:** Use a sequence of pouring between the jars.
* **Explanation:** 
    1. Fill the 5L jar to the top. *(You have 5L and 0L)*
    2. Pour water from the 5L jar into the 3L jar until the 3L jar is full. *(You now have 2L in the big jar and 3L in the small jar).*
    3. Empty the 3L jar completely. *(You have 2L and 0L).*
    4. Pour the 2L of water from the big jar into the small jar. *(You have 0L and 2L).*
    5. Fill the 5L jar to the top again. *(You have 5L and 2L).*
    6. Carefully pour water from the 5L jar into the 3L jar until it is full. Since the 3L jar already has 2L in it, it will only take exactly **1L** to fill it. 
    7. Because you poured exactly 1L out of the 5L jar, you are now left with exactly **4 Litres** in the big jar!
