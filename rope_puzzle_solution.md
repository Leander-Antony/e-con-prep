# Rope Burning Puzzle Solution

**Problem:** You are trying to cook an egg for exactly fifteen minutes, but instead of a timer, you are given two ropes which burn for exactly 1 hour each. The ropes, however, are of uneven densities - i.e., half the rope length-wise might take only two minutes to burn. How can you cook the egg for exactly fifteen minutes?
Note: You should not fold the ropes. You can't measure length of rope and can't cut the rope.

## Solution

This is a classic logic puzzle! The key is understanding that if you light a 1-hour rope from *both* ends simultaneously, it will burn out in exactly half its total time (30 minutes), regardless of its uneven density. 

Here are the step-by-step instructions to measure exactly 15 minutes:

1. **The Initial Setup (0 minutes):**
   * Light **both ends** of Rope A.
   * Simultaneously, light **one end** of Rope B.

2. **Wait for 30 minutes:**
   * Watch Rope A. Because it's burning from both ends, it will completely burn out in exactly 30 minutes.
   * *What's happening to Rope B?* Rope B has been burning from one end for 30 minutes. Therefore, it has exactly 30 minutes of burning time left.

3. **Start the 15-minute Egg Timer (At the 30-minute mark):**
   * The exact moment Rope A completely burns out, **start cooking your egg**.
   * Immediately light the **other (unlit) end** of Rope B.

4. **Wait for 15 minutes:**
   * Rope B has 30 minutes of burn time remaining. However, because it is now burning from *both* ends, it will burn twice as fast.
   * It will take exactly half of its remaining time (30 minutes / 2) to completely turn to ash.
   * When Rope B completely burns out, exactly 15 minutes have passed since you put the egg in. Take the egg off the heat!
