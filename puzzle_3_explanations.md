# Puzzle 3 Explanations

Here are the answers and explanations for the 15 riddles found in `Puzzle 3.docx`:

**1. What goes up the chimney down, but can't go down the chimney up?**
* **Answer:** An umbrella.
* **Explanation:** You can carry a closed (down) umbrella up through a chimney, but you cannot pull an open (up) umbrella down through it because it would get stuck.

**2. It happens once in a minute, twice in a week, and once in a year? What is it?**
* **Answer:** The letter 'e'.
* **Explanation:** Count the 'e's in the words: minüt**e** (1), w**ee**k (2), y**e**ar (1).

**3. What has four legs in the evening, two legs in the afternoon, and three legs in the evening?**
* **Answer:** A human.
* **Explanation:** This is a slight variation/typo of the classic "Riddle of the Sphinx" which usually says "morning" instead of the first "evening". It represents the stages of life: crawling on all fours as a baby (morning/early life), walking on two legs as an adult (afternoon/mid-life), and using a cane as a third leg in old age (evening/late life). 

**4. Imagine that you need to put a coin into an empty bottle and then insert a cork into the neck. Is there a way to remove the coin without taking out the cork or breaking the bottle?**
* **Answer:** Push the cork into the bottle.
* **Explanation:** Since you can't take the cork *out*, you can push it *in* and then simply shake the coin out of the neck.

**5. I live, yet don't breathe, I am freezing. I am never thirsty, but I always drink. WHAT AM I?**
* **Answer:** A fish.
* **Explanation:** Fish are alive but do not breathe air (they extract oxygen from water), they are cold-blooded, and they are constantly taking in water. (This is very similar to Gollum's riddle in *The Hobbit*).

**6. I am a vicious man-killer when I am angry but I am gentle and calm when I am happy. I can be a home pet but my fangs don't suit the job. I have a legend about me but I know it might not be true. I may look scary but I am not really that scary. I may walk into your garden but then I would tell a lie. What am I?**
* **Answer:** A snake (or specifically a garden snake).
* **Explanation:** Some snakes are dangerous ("man-killer", venomous fangs), while others are kept as pets. There are many legends about them. However, a snake cannot "walk" into your garden because it has no legs—so claiming to walk is a "lie." 

**7. When you don't have it you want it, but when you do have it you want to share it, but you have to keep it. What is it?**
* **Answer:** A secret.
* **Explanation:** When you are left out, you want to know the secret. When you know it, human nature makes you want to gossip/share it. However, to truly be a secret, you must keep it to yourself.

**8. What has three feet, but no toes?**
* **Answer:** A yardstick (or a yard).
* **Explanation:** In imperial measurements, there are 3 feet in 1 yard.

**9. What's black and white and red (read) all over?**
* **Answer:** A newspaper.
* **Explanation:** It is printed in black and white ink, and it is "read" (sounds like the color red) by people.

**10. Mary's father has five daughters: 1. Nana, 2. Nene, 3. Nini, 4. Nono. What is the name of the fifth daughter?**
* **Answer:** Mary.
* **Explanation:** The riddle states right at the beginning that it is "Mary's father". Therefore, Mary is one of the daughters.

**11. Give me food, and I will live; give me water, and I will die. What am I?**
* **Answer:** Fire.
* **Explanation:** Fire needs "food" (fuel/wood) to keep burning, but throwing water on it will extinguish ("kill") it.

**12. I'm full of air but can't breathe. What am I?**
* **Answer:** A balloon (or a tire).
* **Explanation:** Both are inflated with air but are inanimate objects.

**13. What gets whiter the dirtier that it gets?**
* **Answer:** A chalkboard (or a blackboard).
* **Explanation:** The more you write on a traditional blackboard with chalk (making it "dirty" with dust), the whiter it becomes.

**14. My first is in tea but not in leaf, My second is in teapot and also in teeth, My third is in caddy but not in cosy, My fourth is in cup but not in rosy, My fifth is in herbal and also in health, My sixth is in peppermint and always in wealth, My last is in drink, so what can I be? I’m there in a classroom, do you listen to me?**
* **Answer:** A TEACHER.
* **Explanation:** This is an acrostic puzzle:
    * **T** (in Tea, not in leaf)
    * **E** (in tEapot and tEEth)
    * **A** (in cAddy, not in cosy)
    * **C** (in Cup, not in rosy)
    * **H** (in Herbal and HealtH)
    * **E** (in pEppErmint and wEalth)
    * **R** (in dRink)
    * A "TEACHER" is in a classroom and you should listen to them!

**15. As fast as an eagle, as slow as a slug. As tall as a tree, as small as a bug. Sometimes in front, sometimes behind. Never on top, and always aligned. What is it?**
* **Answer:** A shadow.
* **Explanation:** A shadow changes size and speed depending on the light source and the object it is attached to. It can stretch out tall or shrink down small, and it is always aligned with the object but falls on surfaces around it, not directly on top.
